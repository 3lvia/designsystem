Tone of voice {{ description }}

Everything we say should be clear, useful and easy to understand for the customer, regardless of whether they know who we are or what they can expect from us.

This means that we have to think about the following when we write or talk to the customer:

  1. We are ON. We understand the everyday life and the challenges of our customers. We fully understand that the power industry can be confusing both when it comes to language, concepts, who is responsible for what, et cetera. Written dialogue should be perceived as a conversation, not as generic information.
  2. We are human and real. Although Elvia has the role of "monopolist", the company consists of ordinary people. Therefore, we can also relate to our customers, who are also ordinary people. We have understanding and empathy, and we talk to them in a friendly and open way.
  3. We translate. The language of the power industry is often complex and confusing, and only experts can make the difficult easy. That is why we work to make the online industry more understandable and increase our customers’ knowledge. We do this through clear and simple, but not stupefying, communication.
  4. We have a twinkle in our eye. Even though we have monopoly and an important role in society we are by no means mundane. Elvia has a fair and subtle kind of humor; we rather give a small wink than shout. We are never condescending or exclusive and we include the customer.

A tone may vary depending on the situation you’re in. You may use a completely different tone when talking to friends than when talking to your boss. The tone can also change depending on the emotional state of the person you are talking to.

When we talk or write with a customer, it is therefore important that we look at the customer's situation - is it a crisis or is it a general question? Is the customer happy or angry? A rule of thumb may be that you do not use the same tone to a customer who is scared or sad than for a customer who is happy and satisfied.

Elvia's tone is professional, but never strictly so. We are clear and speak the customer's language. Put yourself in the customer's situation when formulating yourself. Why is the customer confused? Is the customer impatient because he or she aren’t getting an answer fast enough? Once you have settled some thoughts about where the customer is in the emotional register, you can adjust your tone accordingly. A rule of thumb is to meet the customer where they are.

In summary, we say that our tone can change, but our voice is the same. Elvia’s tone is professional, but friendly and instructive. You can read more about Elvia’s style and tone on brand.elvia.io.
