The purpose of Elvia's brand and visual identity is to appear clear and comprehensive to our customers, partners, employees and society at large.

As a brand, Elvia is ON and activated. In the same way as the light bulb turns on in the bedside table lamp, or the mobile phone is charged when connected to the socket, Elvia is seamlessly present.

The name Elvia came from the norwegian words el and via. The name explains that we supply over 2 million Norwegians with electricity every single day - we make sure that you are on in an electric everyday life.

As Elvia, we are:
  • Proper
    We are honest and responsible in our business operations. We keep our promises and are thorough and professional in our work.
  • Open
    We are welcoming and listening. We cooperate, share our knowledge, and help each other to success.
  • Fearless
    We are brave, energetic and dare to try. We will always seek improvement.

These values must be a starting point for all communication - both internally and externally - and a cornerstone for our tone of voice. The values must be reflected in our corporate culture and be part of our operational work every day.

A big part of the Elvia brand is translated visually through rounded forms, curves and circles. We use green details to communicate that we are active and on. Elvia has an energy ring that communicates that we deliver energy and light and that it has ripple effects for society.

The energy ring defines an area around the logo and/or light circle and can vary in size. The energy ring should never be inside the protection area of the logo (see the example below, where the area is defined in green).

When the energy ring is placed on the same surface as an "OFF text", the line thickness of the ring and the outline of the text should be the same.

The light circle around the logo (seen to the right in the image above) is flexible and can be enlarged. When the circle is enlarged, the center point should always be centered in the “A-dot” in the logo. The circle of light should never be smaller than in the examples above.

The Elvia curve stems from our energy ring and is commonly used for content division, such as in footers or dividers. In our design language, we use green details to contrast from the otherwise dominantly black and white design. This could for example be a green detail in icons or illustrations, or when incorporated in text styles.
