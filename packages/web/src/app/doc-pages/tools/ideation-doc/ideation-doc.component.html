Note: This method is originally made for 6 participants, but can of course be done with less. If you’re a larger group, try splitting up into multiple smaller groups.

  1. Give all participants an A4 sheet of paper and instruct them to divide it into a 3x5 grid (draw two vertical and four horizontal lines over the paper)
  2. Set a timer for 5 minutes and ask participants to fill out the top three empty boxes with separate ideas
  3. Pass all papers to the right and set a new timer for 5 minutes. This time participants take inspiration from the previous ideas and continue filling out the next row of empty boxes.
  4. Repeat this pattern until all boxes are full. In the end, discuss your favorites together.

Tip! Remember that participants can choose themselves if they want to develop the previous ideas, of if they choose to simply add new ones. All ideas are good ideas!

Note: Although brainstorm is a loose term used widely, you can easily elevate your results by approaching them with structure and making sure everyone in the group can contribute.

  1. Define a theme for the brainstorm.
  2. Set a timer and let participants individually write down as many ideas as they can.
  3. When the timer is up, let participants take turns presenting their ideas (and remember to save the critical assessment until everyone’s done presenting). After this, analyze your results and narrow your ideas by using an evaluation method.

Tip! Use using different approaches to achieve different results. One approach could be to ask participants to eliminate all constraints by thinking in the realm of unlimited possibilities, or you could actively choose to brainstorm bad ideas only (and later ask how you can flip them).
